01一蘭 博多駅前店

一人用の仕切りがある「味集中カウンター」が特徴の一蘭は、外国人観光客にも大人気。秘伝の赤いタレが絶品で、辛さや麺の硬さを細かくカスタマイズできます。英語メニューもあるので、語学に自信がなくても安心してご利用いただけます。

02shin-shin 博多デイトス店

博多駅直結のデイトス内にあり、アクセス抜群のshin-shin。あっさりとした博多豚骨に独自のアレンジを加えた「博多ラーメン」は地元ファンが多い一品です。ランチタイムは行列ができることも多いため、時間に余裕を持ってご来店ください。

03元祖長浜屋

福岡ラーメンの原点ともいえる「長浜系」を代表する名店。替え玉文化発祥の地とも言われており、麺だけを追加注文するスタイルが楽しめます。濃厚でありながらすっきりしたスープが癖になる一杯です。

昭和30年代から続く老舗の味を守り続けている博多だるま。濃厚な豚骨スープと細麺の組み合わせは、博多ラーメンの王道スタイル。行列ができる日も多く、それだけの価値がある一杯です。

Top 5 Ramen Spots Near Hakata Station — A Guide for Visitors to Fukuoka

If you're visiting Fukuoka, ramen is non-negotiable. The city's rich, creamy tonkotsu (pork bone) broth is unlike anything you'll find anywhere else in Japan. Here are five highly recommended ramen restaurants all within easy reach of Hakata Station.

01Ichiran Hakata Ekimae

Ichiran is perhaps the most internationally famous ramen chain from Fukuoka, and for good reason. Each diner gets a private booth — the "Flavor Concentration Counter" — allowing you to eat at your own pace without distraction. English menus are available, and you can customize your broth richness, noodle firmness, and spice level to perfection.

02Shin-Shin Hakata Daitos

Located directly inside Hakata Daitos mall — attached to Hakata Station — Shin-Shin is as convenient as it gets. Their lighter, refined take on Hakata tonkotsu has earned a devoted local following. Expect a queue at lunchtime; it's worth the wait.

03Ganso Nagahamaya

Said to be the birthplace of the "kaedama" tradition — ordering extra noodles to finish your remaining broth — Nagahamaya is a living piece of Fukuoka food history. The broth is rich but surprisingly clean on the palate, and the thin noodles cook in seconds.

04Hakata Ikkousha Daitos

Ikkousha is famous for its "foamy tonkotsu" — a broth whipped to a creamy, airy texture that coats the noodles beautifully. It's a gentler introduction to Hakata ramen for those trying tonkotsu for the first time, while still satisfying for seasoned fans.

05Hakata Daruma

A classic that has been serving Fukuoka's definitive tonkotsu since the 1950s. Hakata Daruma keeps things simple: ultra-rich broth, thin firm noodles, and no shortcuts. Lines form here regularly, and every minute of waiting is repaid in the bowl.
